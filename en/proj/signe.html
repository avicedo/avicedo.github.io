Signe magazine

Signe magazine is an annual publication that deals with current affairs in all branches of design with a focus on graphic design. It's a curricular project carried out by the last year students of the EASD d'Alcoi and it's done with a general theme that covers the whole magazine. In its tenth edition the theme chosen was the search for equality and the concept of design as a democratizing tool. Issues related to anti-racism, anti-fascism, feminism and functional diversity within the world of design were addressed.
From the very beginning the magazine was articulated using inclusive language and giving importance to the interviewees prefered self-perception.

Within the magazine I developed my activity in the Art Direction department as head of layout design. Together with the team of layout designers we developed a grid based on two page typologies, one with three columns and the other with two. Special emphasis was placed on modernizing the Van De Graaf page canon, using it as a base.
From this point on, we decided to use the Literata typeface for the body of the text due to its ease of reading. We combined it with Clash Grotesk to create contrast and modernize the image of the page.

The combination of reticle, typefaces and the wonderful work of the team culminated in a modern-looking magazine that also takes into account visual accessibility thanks to its classic, readability-focused origin. The magazine is available from EASD d'Alcoi.
