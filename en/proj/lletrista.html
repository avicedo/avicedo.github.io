Recuperación tipográfica

I have loved letterforms since I was a child. The first memory I have of appreciating good lettering is with the sign of "El Sulfato Atómico", by Francisco Ibáñez.
The second is looking at old bussiness signs on road trips. Although they were usually abandoned, the facades of the old buildings still had typographic decorations with colors and shapes that were still vivid and bright despite the passing of time.

Over the years, this fascination has turned into concern, as I see the graphic imagery of cities that I saw as a child disappearing. I witnessed the proliferation of big chains and neighborhood stores closing. And with them I saw their signs disappear. As a result of this and after learning about Juan Nava's "Letras Recuperadas" I decided to start my own project, photographing signs and posters that stood out to me.

The project really connected with me when I joined the Red Ibérica en Defensa del Patrimonio Gráfico where I also began to vectorize these signs. The project has also evolved to other areas such as the recovery of the Valencian tile.
Nowadays I share my vectorizations mainly on Instagram
